# Marketing & Go-To-Market Guide

> How to get PracticeVoice AI in front of practices and turn it into revenue.
> Written for someone brand new to selling software. Follow it in order.

---

## The one sentence that sells it

> "You're probably missing 1 in 4 phone calls — and each one is a $300 patient
> walking to your competitor. PracticeVoice AI answers every call 24/7, books
> the appointment, and shows you exactly how much revenue it brought in."

Memorize that. It's your whole pitch.

---

## 1. Who to sell to (pick ONE to start)

Don't sell to "everyone." Pick a single niche, get great at it, then expand.
Ranked best-to-start-with:

1. **Dental practices** ⭐ (recommended first target)
   - They live and die by their appointment book.
   - High no-show/missed-call pain, clear per-appointment dollar value.
   - Lots of them, easy to find, owner usually makes the decision.
2. **Med spas / cosmetic / dermatology** — cash-pay, marketing-savvy, value ROI.
3. **Small law firms** (family, personal injury, immigration) — one missed
   client call can be worth thousands; lawyers get this instantly.
4. **Independent medical / chiropractic / physical therapy** — good, but more
   insurance complexity.

**Start with dental.** Everything below assumes dental; swap the words for other
niches later.

### Your ideal first customer
- **1–3 locations** (small enough that the owner decides fast, big enough to
  feel the pain).
- Owner-operated (you talk to the person who signs the check).
- Already spends on marketing (they understand ROI).

Avoid: giant multi-location groups (slow, committee decisions) for now.

---

## 2. The core message (why they buy)

People don't buy "AI." They buy outcomes. Lead with these three, in this order:

1. **Stop losing money** — "Every missed call is lost revenue." (fear)
2. **Never miss a call again** — 24/7, instant, warm, professional. (relief)
3. **See the ROI** — the dashboard proves the dollars. (proof)

Your unfair advantage vs. competitors (Smith.ai, Ruby, generic answering
services): **you show the revenue the AI generated.** Nobody else makes the
money this obvious. Hammer that.

## 4. Where to find customers (channels, cheapest first)

### A. Direct outreach (start here — free, fastest feedback)
1. Build a list of 50 local dental practices (Google Maps + their websites).
2. **Call them** (yes, ironic). Or email the owner. Or walk in.
3. Goal of first contact: book a **10-minute screen-share demo**, not a sale.

### B. The demo *is* the sales tool
You already have a live, beautiful demo. On the call:
1. Show the homepage + the live-call animation.
2. Log in, show the **Dashboard** — point at "Revenue Generated: $74,160."
3. Show a **Call Detail** transcript — "this is what every call looks like."
4. Say: "Let's set up your clinic right now, it takes 5 minutes." (Settings page)

Seeing it books the deal. Don't over-explain the tech.

### C. Scale-up channels (once you have 2–3 paying customers)
- **Local Facebook groups** for dentists / practice managers.
- **Dental Facebook & LinkedIn ads** targeting practice owners.
- **Referrals** — ask happy customers for one intro each. Offer a free month.
- **Dental office managers on LinkedIn** — they feel the missed-call pain daily.
- **Partner with dental consultants / marketing agencies** who already sell to
  practices; give them a cut.

## 5. Cold outreach templates (copy-paste, then personalize)

### Email
> **Subject: quick question about [Practice Name]'s phones**
>
> Hi Dr. [Last name],
>
> Quick one — when your front desk is busy or closed, what happens to the calls?
>
> Most practices miss 20–30% of them, and each missed call can be a $300+
> patient. I built an AI receptionist that answers every call 24/7, books the
> appointment straight into your calendar, and texts a confirmation — then shows
> you exactly how much revenue it booked.
>
> Can I show you a 10-minute demo this week? First 14 days are free, no card.
>
> — [Your name]

### Voicemail / phone opener
> "Hi, this is [name]. I help dental practices stop losing patients to missed
> calls — my AI answers 24/7 and books appointments automatically. I'd love to
> show you a 10-minute demo. My number is ___."

### Instagram/Facebook DM (short)
> "Love what you're doing at [Practice]! Quick q — are you catching all your
> after-hours calls? I've got an AI receptionist that books those appointments
> automatically and shows the revenue. Worth a 10-min look?"

**Rule:** every message ends with ONE ask — book the demo.

## 6. Handling the 5 objections you'll always hear

- **"Will it sound like a robot?"** → "Modern AI voices are warm and natural —
  let me play you one." (Then show the demo.)
- **"Is it HIPAA-ready?"** → "We're built for HIPAA-ready workflows with encryption
  and a Business Associate Agreement on qualifying plans." (See the HIPAA page.)
- **"My staff already answers the phone."** → "Perfect — this catches the calls
  they *can't*: lunch, after hours, when two lines ring at once."
- **"It's too expensive."** → "One booked cleaning covers the month. Everything
  after that is profit. Try it free for 14 days and watch the dashboard."
- **"I need to think about it."** → "Totally fair — the trial is free and takes
  5 minutes to set up. Let's start it now so you have real numbers to think
  *with*."

## 7. Your first 30 days (a concrete plan)

**Week 1 — Learn & prep**
- Read `01-HOW-IT-WORKS.md`. Play with the live demo until you can drive it in
  your sleep.
- Build a list of 50 local dental practices.

**Week 2 — Outreach**
- Contact all 50 (call/email/DM). Aim for 5 booked demos.
- Refine your pitch after each one.

**Week 3 — Demos & first trials**
- Run the demos. Start free trials for anyone interested.
- Connect the real plumbing (see `03-LAUNCH-CHECKLIST.md`) for your first
  serious prospect.

**Week 4 — Close & learn**
- Convert trials to paid. Ask every happy user for one referral.
- Goal: **1–3 paying customers.** That's a real business starting.

## 8. Metrics to track (so you know it's working)

- **Demos booked per week** (activity)
- **Demo → trial rate** (is the pitch landing?)
- **Trial → paid rate** (is the product delivering?)
- **Monthly recurring revenue (MRR)** = your customers × their plan price
- **Churn** (do they stay? if not, why?)

Simple goal math: **10 Professional customers = $3,490/month recurring.**
30 customers = ~$10k/month. That's the ladder.
